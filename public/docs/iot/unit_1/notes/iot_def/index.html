    IoT DEFINITIONS AND FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS#

    Introduction#

    The term Internet of Things (IoT) has been defined in many ways by researchers, industries, and standard bodies. Different definitions focus on different aspects such as sensing, connectivity, intelligence, or applications, which has led to conceptual ambiguity. To overcome this, Honbo Zhou analyzes multiple IoT definitions and extracts their common characteristics and functional requirements.

    IoT is not a single technology but an evolutionary integration of existing ICT technologies, enabling physical objects to be digitally represented, interconnected, and intelligently managed.

    Definitions of Internet of Things (IoT)#

    General Definition#

    The Internet of Things refers to a paradigm in which physical objects (“things”) are instrumented with sensing, identification, and communication capabilities, allowing them to interact with each other and with information systems over networks.

    IoT extends the Internet from connecting people and information to connecting things and real-world processes.

    IoT as an Evolution of ICT#

    According to the reference text, IoT is:

    • An aggregation of existing technologies

    • A convergence of sensing, communication, and computing

    • An evolution of traditional ICT systems

    Technologies such as RFID, WSN, M2M, SCADA, middleware, and cloud computing together form the technological foundation of IoT.

    IoT as an Umbrella Concept#

    IoT is often confused with related terms:

    • RFID – identification of objects

    • WSN – sensing and data collection

    • M2M – device-to-device communication

    • SCADA – monitoring and control

    IoT acts as an umbrella framework that integrates all these technologies into a single, unified system.

    Common Characteristics of IoT Systems#

    Despite varied definitions, most IoT interpretations agree on two fundamental sets of characteristics: 3I and 5A.

    3I Characteristics of IoT#

    1. Instrumented#

    Physical objects are equipped with:

    • Sensors

    • Actuators

    • RFID tags

    This enables data generation from the physical world.

    2. Interconnected#

    Objects are connected using:

    • Wired networks

    • Wireless networks

    This allows data exchange between devices and systems.

    3. Intelligent#

    Collected data is processed and analyzed to:

    • Generate information

    • Support decision making

    • Enable automation

    The 3I model forms the technical foundation of IoT.

    5A Characteristics of IoT#

    IoT systems aim to provide services:

    • Anything

    • Anywhere

    • Anytime

    • Anyway

    • Anyhow

    The 5A model highlights the ubiquitous and pervasive nature of IoT applications.

    Functional Requirements of IoT#

    Based on the above characteristics, IoT systems must satisfy the following functional requirements.

    1. Sensing and Data Acquisition#

    IoT systems must be able to sense and collect data from the physical environment using:

    • Sensors

    • RFID tags

    • Embedded devices

    This enables real-world conditions to be digitally represented.

    2. Connectivity and Communication#

    IoT systems require reliable communication through:

    • Wired networks

    • Wireless networks

    • Short-range and long-range technologies

    Connectivity is essential for data transmission and integration.

    3. Identification and Addressability#

    Each “thing” must be:

    • Uniquely identifiable

    • Addressable within the network

    This enables tracking, management, and interaction with large numbers of objects.

    4. Data Processing and Intelligence#

    Raw sensor data must be:

    • Stored

    • Processed

    • Analyzed

    This converts data into useful information and actionable intelligence, supporting automation and decision making.

    5. Monitoring and Control#

    IoT systems must support:

    • Real-time monitoring

    • Event detection

    • Remote control and actuation

    This requirement is critical for industrial, infrastructure, and smart-city applications.

    6. Middleware and Integration#

    Due to heterogeneity of devices and networks, IoT systems require a middleware layer to:

    • Integrate diverse technologies

    • Provide unified interfaces

    • Enable interoperability

    Middleware acts as the glue of IoT systems.

    7. Scalability and Manageability#

    IoT systems must support:

    • Large-scale deployment

    • Dynamic addition and removal of devices

    • Remote and centralized management

    Scalability is essential for real-world IoT applications.

    8. Security and Privacy#

    IoT systems must ensure:

    • Secure communication

    • Authentication and authorization

    • Protection of sensitive data

    Security is a fundamental requirement due to the distributed nature of IoT.

    Relationship Between Definitions and Functional Requirements#

    Although IoT definitions vary, they converge when viewed through functional requirements. Every IoT system must:

    • Sense the physical world

    • Connect objects via networks

    • Process data intelligently

    • Deliver meaningful services

    Functional requirements provide a practical understanding of IoT beyond theoretical definitions.

    Conclusion#

    The Internet of Things is a broad and evolving paradigm with multiple definitions emphasizing different aspects. However, as explained in the reference text, these definitions converge around common characteristics and functional requirements. By focusing on instrumentation, interconnection, intelligence, and ubiquitous service availability, IoT systems bridge the physical and digital worlds. Understanding IoT through its definitions and functional requirements provides a strong foundation for designing scalable, interoperable, and intelligent IoT systems.
